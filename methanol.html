Aspen Hysys Simulation of Methanol Production via Carbon Dioxide Hydrogenation

Methanol is one of the most highly versatile chemicals widely used for chemical synthesis that benefited different industries such as construction, automotive, electronics, and energy generation. Its derivatives are used for building up a vast number of compounds. Large quantities of methanol are converted to dimethylaniline for dyes and formaldehyde for synthetic resins. Numerous pieces of literature published the forecasted demand for methanol, and it is expected to increase from 61 million metric tons in 2012 to 146 million metric tons in 2022. One of the major factors driving the market studied is the increasing demand for methanol-based fuel. The rising demand for methanol-based fuels is likely to act as an opportunity in the future as it requires more methanol production plant for global consumption. Recently, research has begun using pure CO2 and a separate pure source of H2 as starting materials for methanol synthesis. Laureate George Olah proposed the patented process that uses CO2 and hydrogen as feedstock and converts into renewable methanol, with oxygen as the only by-product. Overall, the proposed plant is designed to produce 99.98 % methanol purity using an efficient process for methanol synthesis through CO2 hydrogenation using wet hydrogen by-product from a Chlor-alkali production plant using Cu/Zn/Al/Zr fibrous catalyst. The optimized process was simulated through Aspen HYSYS, allowing the proposed plant to be designed efficiently and effectively.
